Driving Sustainability: Environmental Protection in Malaysia

With its diverse geography, Malaysia is tasked with addressing unique environmental concerns while balancing economic growth.

Over recent years, government bodies have ramped up their funding and initiatives to address everything from biodiversity protection to pollution management.

Breakdown of Expenditure on Environmental Protection by State

Malaysia's commitment to funding environmental protection remains strong as efforts are highly focused on waste and pollution management.

While progress has been made, the future of our environment relies on continued focus and strategic investment in diversified sustainability efforts.

The ASEAN region faces the dual challenge of fostering economic growth while minimising its environmental impact.

As the region progresses, understanding the dynamics of carbon output will be crucial to shaping a sustainable and equitable future for all.

Malaysia has a higher dependence on fossil fuels than its ASEAN peers. Shifts in energy policies like the Corporate Renewable Energy Supply Scheme (CRESS) influence the energy mix.
